Project Mission

CareBot is committed to providing an AI-powered platform that helps caregivers, social workers, and families quickly find reliable local resources before they reach a crisis. We crowdsource resource recommendations, moderate submissions, and maintain a high-quality database of Alabama healthcare and social services.

Problems We Are Solving

  • Resource discovery: Caregivers and families struggle to find local healthcare resources when they need them.
  • Data quality: Community-submitted resources need validation, deduplication, and structured merge.
  • Rural access: Gaps exist in counties with limited or scattered services.
  • Trust & moderation: Submissions must be reviewed and integrated in a controlled, auditable way.

Sprint 1 Goals

Sprint 1 focused on rebuilding the resource submission and moderation system. A significant portion of the sprint was spent researching and familiarizing with Docker, which the team used for local development and deployment. We prioritized fixing the public submission form, implementing merge functionality with field-level deduplication, improving Docker portability across Mac and Windows, and applying initial security hardening. The team also produced documentation and setup guides to support onboarding.

Sprint 1 Retrospective

What Went Well

  • Research and familiarization with Docker paid off — environment eventually built and ran across Mac and Windows
  • Regular scrum meetings kept the team aligned
  • Agreed on a single fork as a group
  • Merge flow with approve, merge, reject and audit logging completed
  • Documentation (CHANGES.md, BUILD_AND_TEST.md) supported onboarding
  • Security hardening completed as planned

What Didn't Go Well

  • Initial Docker build failures, credential errors, missing .env — research and learning curve took significant sprint time
  • Some team members lacked write access to GitHub early on
  • Challenges understanding the inherited submission code
  • Limited familiarity with Django, PostGIS, pgvector
